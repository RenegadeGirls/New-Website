What?

The Renegade Girls Tinkering Club runs exciting summer camps in San Francisco for elementary school kids. We founded the Renegade Girls Tinkering Club to inspire girls and underrepresented people to love tinkering and making! We partner with Summer Together to enable low income kids to attend our camp for free, and fundraise for a sliding scale program.

Where?

We run our popular summer camp in San Francisco, CA.

When?

Renegade Girls has run summer camps, holiday camps, and after school programming since 2015. Each summer’s camp dates are found in our Programs tab.

Why?

A girl-only environment to provide peer support, where every girl’s contribution is valuable and heard! It's no secret that women are tragically underrepresented in STEM fields. Girls learn from a young age that they can step confidently into STEM fields. Our coed programs welcome kids from underrepresented groups to also open the door to STEM in a supportive environment.

How?

We learn by doing with our hands, building, trying, designing, and repeating. Tinkering is the act of taking something apart in order to learn how it works. Then, putting it back together and improving on its design. Hands-on with trial and error. Experiential learning.

Employment

The Renegade Girls Tinkering Club offers fun and community for children to explore the world of tinkering and making. Children get hands-on with their peers to see how things work. Tinkering, a process involving hands-on discovery, and trial and error toward a goal, helps girls develop grit and gives a solid foundation for STEM skills. Founded by mothers in STEM careers working to change the vast gender imbalance in their fields, the Renegade Girls Tinkering Club inspires girls to keep their love of scientific exploration.

Summer Camp Directory

We are looking for a Camp Director for our Summer Camps to supervise our camp counselors and make operations happen seamlessly. What do Camp Directors do? The most important out of all Camp Director duties is to ensure camp staff are working effectively, and that all campers are safe at all times. The job duties of a Camp Director include supervising camp staff, scheduling substitutes when a staffer is unavailable, ensuring that the staff is operating effectively, that the campers are happy, and that the site is cleaned up each day. Being good at resolving interpersonal conflicts and able to build good relationships with adults, teens, and children are two essential traits of a Camp Director. You need to have knowledge around helping children to resolve differences and how to motivate staff. If you’re experienced teaching elementary school children and have good relationships with your coworkers, we’d like to meet you.

Responsibilities
  • Lead all camp staff, including camp counselors and assistants
  • Oversee camp operations, including staffing and setup and cleanup of classrooms
  • Educate camp counselors on how to resolve differences between campers
  • Set up and clean up the space at the beginning and end of summer (ensuring everything is in the same condition or better than when you arrived)
  • Resolve occasional fights between children and address behavioral issues
  • Make sure all safety rules are followed
  • Perform minor first aid as needed
  • Keep accurate incident logs
Skills
  • Proven experience as Camp Director, Teacher, or similar role working with groups of children
  • Patient and reliable
  • Organizational and communication skills
  • A love for children
  • Energetic and positive personality
  • Ability to meet the physical demands of the job (strength and stamina to follow children's pace)
  • First aid and CPR-certified preferred
  • College degree
